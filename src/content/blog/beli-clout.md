If I have no other accomplishments to tell, then at least let me say this: I have Beli clout. 

What is Beli clout? you ask. Let me tell you. 

First, *what even is Beli?* Beli is an app where you can rank and review restaurants, but the catch is you only see your friends' rankings and reviews. It feels more personal. How do you know if that person’s 1 star review from Google Maps is simply because they are having a bad day or if the food is actually bad?


I had a bit of a reputation as a foodie even before Beli. My friends and I would make food instagrams and tag each other as we rate food. As Beli got bigger, 5 friends told me to join before I finally, albeit somewhat reluctantly, join the app. Immediately, I spammed ratings in Boston and New York to memory dump where I have been and what I thought of it. As I try out more restaurants in New York, I have climbed up the rankings. As of July 2026, I am rank 387 nationally aka I ranked 387th most restaurants on Beli. It’s something my friends used to introduce to me, such as “This is Kristin, you have to see her Beli”.
 
The amount of restaurants is probably a reason why. The other is the reviews I write. I would write what I do and do not like, and sprinkle in some personal life happenings there. Let me show you a recent review from Beli (forgive my grammar): 

> “First stop of our Charles river bike tour! Looks promising but underwhelming, a foreshadowing of the rest of the stops I fear. The “cream puff” is a melon pan w/ cream, the yuzu and matcha cream itself has the flavors it’s supposed to have but I have more one side yuzu and the other side more match. The panda cotta matcha felt closer to culinary grade than ceremonial grade.”

It’s nowhere nearly as descriptive as a food critic’s (and let’s be fair, I don’t get paid to do this), but it’s honest and gets the vibe. Just the number ranking (ex. 6.0) I find it very subjective, so words matter much more.

The result? My friend told me her classmate places more emphasis on my review than others. My sister’s friends requested to follow me (because my sister told them about how I rank a lot of restaurants). 

_So…this is what I learned about B2B Saas…_

I am just kidding, but it is interesting to see how my private beli page grew from word of mouth. Now that my sister and her friends follow me, I have to be semi-careful about what I post so they don’t get a negative impression of me. 

I ask myself if I would want that Beli clout to transfer over to an influencer clout. While I would certainly love to make the same amount of money as an influencer, I like that it’s a closer circle of food reviews and therefore trust. I have 0 influence outside of my friends, so why would a restaurant comp my meal for an influence of 10? 

Sometimes I would ask myself, maybe there should be a way to comp and influence that person because certainly that person holds more weight on their friends minds than an influencer, right? 

However, how much of that influence actually makes? It’s a difference of I want to go vs I must go. I still find out about a certain place through an influencer, but I go debating on if it’s something I am personally interested in trying (such as a new canto spot vs a $100+ Michelin experience). I don’t trust if they say it’s good or not, because they will, subconsciously or consciously, be softer in their review, I just know if it looks interesting. That’s the discovery part. However, if my friends say it’s really good, and I know our tastes are similar, then I will put that place in my mind as higher priority to go, and order the dishes they tried and said are good. In marketing terms, influencers help with discovery and awareness, while a friend's recommendation may go deeper down that funnel into consideration. A friend is a stronger advocate than an influencer, because of that trust component. Maybe that’s also what it takes to stand out in the cut throat NYC scene. I can’t tell you how many friends went to Kinhfolk from my recommendation.

My conclusion would be it’s better for a friend’s recommendation to happen organically rather than to have any programs that incentivizes or comps meals in fear of losing the honest judgment. Beli clout doesn’t pay, but it means you do have trust from your friends, and sometimes friends of friends as well.
